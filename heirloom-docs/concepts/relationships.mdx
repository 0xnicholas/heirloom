---
title: Relationships
description: Heirloom replaces generic graph edges with three precise relationship semantics.
---

Heirloom replaces the generic "edge" of a traditional graph database with three precise relationship types. Each type has well-defined lifecycle, deletion, permission, and ownership semantics.

This lets agents understand the consequences of a write operation before they perform it.

## Relationship types

| Type | Lifecycle dependency | On delete | Permission propagation | Ownership transfer |
|------|----------------------|-----------|------------------------|--------------------|
| **Ownership** | Dependent | Owner deleted → dependent cascaded | Owner's capability propagates downward | Yes |
| **Reference** | Independent | Referenced deleted → reference breaks | None | No |
| **Association** | Independent | Either side deleted → other side unchanged | None | No |

## Ownership

An Ownership relationship means one resource controls the lifecycle of another.

- Deleting the owner cascades to the owned resource.
- Capabilities granted on the owner can propagate downward.
- Ownership can be explicitly transferred through a governed Action.

Example: A `Customer` owns `Address` resources. Deleting the customer also deletes its addresses.

## Reference

A Reference relationship is a pointer. The referenced resource has its own independent lifecycle.

- Deleting the referenced resource breaks the reference.
- The break is detectable and can trigger alerts.
- The referencing resource continues to exist.

Example: An `Order` references a `Product`. The product can be discontinued without deleting the order, but the order now points to a stale reference.

## Association

An Association relationship links two independent resources.

- Either side can be deleted without affecting the other.
- No permission propagation.
- No ownership transfer.

Example: A `Vendor` and a `CertificationBody` are associated through a partnership. Removing one does not remove the other.

## Agent benefit

When an agent plans a deletion, it can use relationship semantics to predict cascade consequences. It does not need to infer business rules from training data or query documentation.
